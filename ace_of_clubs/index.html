Ace of Clubs

May 2021 - Aug 2021

What's this game about?

The object is just like real golf: sink the ball in as few strokes as possible. However, in this multiplayer game, players are dealt cards that can be played on others that either help or hinder their golfing game.

Level Design

Each hole was designed to with a fundamental challenge/gimmmick.

1. Gamble

This par 4 encourages the player to cut across the water for a closer approach shot to the green (but risk losing their ball to the lake).

2. Choice

This par 4 has two paths, one with high ground and the other with low ground.

3. Cut

This par 4 offers the player a tough shot of cutting through the trees.

4. Bend

This par 3 doesn't have a straight shot to the green. The player must curve the ball around the tree to stay on par.

5. Long

This par 5 is long, dead straight, and downhill. Save your power shot cards for this hole and smack it!

6. Trap

A greedy player might hit the ball too far and get stuck behind the tree in the dead end on this par 4.

7. Coast

This par 5 can be completed in 4 strokes if the player can pull off a precise drive onto the small island.

8. Bowl

The ball will bounce and rolls towards the hole no matter where it lands in this par 3.

9. Incovenient

There is a river right at the average drive distance. The player must hit a weak drive on this par 4.

C# Scripts

Birdie Trajectory: In this game, the birdie follows a slight variation of simple projectile motion. To account for the large amount of air resistance that a light-weight feather birdie experiences during its flight, its velocity is multiplied by 0.99 every frame (The Oculus runs at 72 FPS).

Racket Ricochet: The badminton racket has a box collider around its head. When a birdie enters this collider, its velocity is set to the normal of the the racket strings multiplied by the speed of the tip of the racket. Notice that the outgoing velocity is not dependent on the ingoing velocity. This is because the power imparted by the racket strings greatly outweights the inertia of the light birdie.
